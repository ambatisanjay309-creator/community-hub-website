Upcoming Events

Community events happening this month across Apex and Wake County.

Did you know? Apex hosts over 60 community events per year — from the weekly Farmers Market to seasonal festivals. Many are free and open to all residents.

Apex Farmers Market — Opening Day

Fresh local produce, artisan goods, baked items, and live music in historic downtown Apex. Every Saturday 8 AM–12 PM through October. Accepts SNAP/EBT — double your dollars with Double Up Food Bucks.

🥬 Food 🎵 Free
📍 Salem Street, Downtown Apex · 8:00 AM–12:00 PM

10

Apex Town Council Regular Meeting

Monthly public meeting of the Apex Town Council. Residents may comment during the public hearing period. Agenda posted 48 hours in advance at apexnc.org. Held the second Tuesday of each month.

🏛 Civic 🆓 Free & Open
📍 Apex Town Hall, 73 Hunter St · 7:00 PM · apexnc.org

14

Wake County Library Summer Reading Kickoff

The annual Summer Reading Program begins at all 20+ Wake County library branches. Kids, teens, and adults can sign up for reading logs, earn prizes, and attend free programs all summer long.

📚 Education 🆓 Free
📍 All WCPL Branches · wakegov.com/libraries

21

Apex Parks Summer Camp Registration Deadline

Last day to register for Apex Parks & Recreation summer day camps for youth ages 5–15. Programs include STEM, sports, arts, and outdoor adventure. Financial assistance available — apply alongside registration.

🌱 Youth 💰 Aid Available
📍 Apex Community Center · (919) 249-3400 · apexnc.org

24

Wake County Housing Forum — Affordable Options

Wake County Human Services hosts a free forum on affordable housing programs, rental assistance, and first-time homebuyer resources. Staff on site to answer questions and assist with applications.

🏠 Housing 🆓 Free
📍 Wake County Human Services, 220 Swinburne St, Raleigh · 1:00–4:00 PM

28

Apex Senior Center Monthly Lunch & Social

Monthly catered lunch and social program at the Apex Senior Center for residents 55+. This month features a local history presentation and chair yoga demo. RSVP requested but not required.

👴 Seniors 🍽 Lunch Included
📍 Apex Senior Center, 53 Hunter St · 11:30 AM · (919) 249-3402

Apex Fourth of July Celebration

Annual Independence Day parade through downtown Apex followed by fireworks at the Sports Complex. One of the largest free community events of the year — arrive early. Live music from 6 PM.

🎆 Festival 🆓 Free
📍 Downtown Apex → Apex Sports Complex · All day · apexnc.org

Apex Town Council Meeting

Regular monthly meeting. July agenda expected to include the annual budget review and a community feedback session on proposed greenway expansion. Open to all residents.

🏛 Civic 🆓 Open

GoTriangle Open House — New Routes

GoTriangle presents proposed changes to regional bus routes serving Apex and Holly Springs. Staff available to answer questions. Spanish-language materials provided. Light refreshments served.

🚌 Transit 🆓 Free
📍 Apex Community Center · 5:30–7:30 PM · gotriangle.org

WCPSS Back-to-School Enrollment Fair

Wake County Public Schools hosts a one-stop enrollment event for new and returning families. Bring proof of address, immunization records, and student ID. Staff available in English and Spanish.

🏫 Education 🌐 Multilingual
📍 Apex Community Center · 9:00 AM–3:00 PM · wcpss.net

Community Health Fair — Free Screenings

Wake County Health & Human Services hosts a free community health fair with blood pressure checks, diabetes screenings, dental referrals, and health insurance enrollment help. No appointment needed.

🏥 Health 🆓 Free
📍 Apex Community Center · 10:00 AM–2:00 PM · (919) 250-3430

Apex Food Pantry Monthly Distribution

Monthly food distribution by the Apex United Methodist Church food pantry, open to any Wake County resident in need. No proof of income required. Distributions typically last until supplies run out.
