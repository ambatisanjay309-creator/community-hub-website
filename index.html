June 2026 — Summer Spotlight

Apex Parks & Recreation Summer Programs

With school out, Apex Parks & Recreation offers summer day camps, swim lessons, sports clinics, and teen programs — many with financial assistance available. Registration fills fast; apply early. The department also coordinates the Apex Senior Center's summer lunch program for residents 55+.

Newcomer Guide

New to Apex?

Welcome to one of North Carolina's most vibrant communities. Here's how to get connected fast.

Welcome to Apex, NC

Apex has been one of the fastest-growing towns in America for good reason — excellent schools, a thriving downtown, safe neighborhoods, and easy access to the Research Triangle. But navigating a new community can be overwhelming.

This hub is your starting point. Below are the first five things every new Apex resident should know.

1

Register Your Kids for School

Wake County Public Schools (WCPSS) enrollment opens online. Visit wcpss.net for your assigned school by address.

2

Get a Free Library Card

The Apex Branch Library on W. Beaver Creek Dr. is free for all Wake County residents — books, digital resources, and Wi-Fi.

3

Save 2-1-1 in Your Phone

NC 211 connects you to any local service — 24/7, free, and available in multiple languages.

4

Learn About GoApex Transit

Apex runs free and low-cost bus routes connecting to GoTriangle regional transit for the broader metro area.

5

Attend a Town Hall Meeting

Apex Town Council meets monthly — a great way to meet neighbors and understand your new community.

🏫

Find Schools & Childcare

GoApex, GoTriangle, and community transportation options

Connect with Town Government

Permits, utilities, parks, and public services at apexnc.org
