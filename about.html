About the Team

Built by Apex High School Students

We are six students from Apex High School who believe technology should serve our community. This site was created for the 2025 North Carolina TSA State Leadership Conference Webmaster event—but we built it to be genuinely useful for the people of Apex and Wake County.

Our Team

The people behind the Hub

Each team member contributed research, design, writing, or development to make this project a reality.

Sanjay Ambati

Project Lead & Lead Developer

Sanjay directed the overall technical architecture of the site, leading front-end development and ensuring every page met TSA competition standards. He is a junior at Apex High with a passion for full-stack web development and computer science.

Ojas Addepalli

UI/UX Designer & CSS Specialist

Ojas designed the visual identity of the site—selecting the color palette, typography system, and layout hierarchy. He built the majority of the shared stylesheet and ensured the site was fully responsive across all screen sizes.

Jeremiah Joseph

Content Researcher & Writer

Jeremiah conducted the community needs research that shaped the site's focus areas. He verified each organization's contact information, wrote the resource descriptions, and authored the Featured Resources page with depth and local authenticity.

Vihaan Shah

Vihaan built the site's interactive features, including the real-time search and filter system on the Resource Directory and the form validation logic on the Submit page. He also led accessibility review to ensure the site met WCAG standards.

Umar engaged directly with the Apex community—surveying peers and family members to understand what services were most needed and least known. His fieldwork shaped the resource selection and the target audience priorities of the site.

Our Mission

Apex is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in North Carolina—and with rapid growth comes a growing need for centralized community information. Too many residents, especially seniors, new families, and students, don't know what resources are available to them right here in Apex and Wake County.

We built this site to change that. Our goal was to create a resource that a student could use to find after-school tutoring, that a parent could use to find a food pantry in an emergency, or that a newcomer could use to learn how to get around on public transit—all in one place, for free.

About Our School

Apex High School is a public high school located in Apex, North Carolina, serving grades 9–12. It is part of the Wake County Public School System and home to over 3,000 students. Apex High has a strong tradition of academic excellence, extracurricular achievement, and community involvement.

Our TSA chapter has participated in the Webmaster competition for several years. This project represents our most ambitious site to date—one we hope will actually benefit our neighbors long after competition season ends.
